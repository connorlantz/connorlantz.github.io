Project: Office Space

You should invest in the places you spend most of your time.

August 5, 2022 Connor Lantz

Scope

Creating the perfect workspace is an art, and it all began when the pandemic pushed me to redesign my environment for optimal productivity. Convinced that I needed a dedicated space to tackle my online university courses, I took over the house's office area, giving me the freedom to craft the ideal study haven.

For virtual learning, I identified key necessities: multiple screens, ample desk space, and adaptability. I employed heavy-duty brackets to support two wooden countertops, purchased from Home Depot. This design not only freed up desk space but also created an organized, clutter-free environment. To maximize screen real estate, I installed two adjustable wall mounts for screens and a laptop wall mount. These adaptable mounts allow me to switch configurations easily, perfect for collaborative study sessions.

Design and Build

Of course, every project has its challenges. Achieving level countertops in a corner desk shape proved to be quite tedious. However, the real test was financial. Making these substantial purchases required justifying the expenses. Yet, I firmly believe in building things right the first time, especially when it comes to a workspace where I'd be spending a significant portion of my life.

Over time, my workspace has continued to evolve. The most significant transformation occurred when I introduced a 3D printer. Thanks to my ample desk space, I seamlessly integrated my 3D printing setup with my study area. Eventually, I relocated my 3D printing station to the basement, optimizing underutilized space. The new setup mirrored my office design, featuring two wooden countertops, shelves for filament storage, and pegboards for organizing tools.

Key take-aways

Investing Wisely: While it was challenging to allocate a significant budget for this project, I learned the value of investing wisely in creating a comfortable and efficient workspace. The benefits of increased productivity and reduced distractions outweighed the initial costs.

Adaptability is Key: The ability to reconfigure my workspace for different tasks, including collaborative work, has been invaluable. It reinforced the importance of adaptability in a dynamic work environment.

My workspace is ever-changing, and it will likely look completely different a year from now. I've recently added hangers for my backpacks and other accessories to enhance organization. Additionally, I plan to purchase a CNC router for potential woodworking projects in my 3D printer space. This ongoing evolution reflects my commitment to continuously optimize my workspace for productivity and comfort.
